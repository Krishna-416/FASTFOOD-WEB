History of Pizza

Pizza originated in Naples, Italy in the 18th century. Before then,
flatbreads were cooked in wood-fired ovens and topped with various ingredients.

Types of Pizza
1. Neapolitan Pizza: Originating from Naples, characterized by a thin crust, soft center, and fresh toppings.

2. New York-Style Pizza: Known for its large, thin, and foldable slices.

3. Chicago-Style Deep-Dish Pizza: A thick crust with a thick layer of cheese, meats, and vegetables.

4. California Pizza: Known for non-traditional toppings and flavor combinations.
